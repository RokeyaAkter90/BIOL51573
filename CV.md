RESEARCH PROFILE
My path into data science and bioinformatics grew out of over eight years of hands-on work as a microbiologist in pharmaceutical and dietary supplement industries. Those years taught me to think carefully about biological data long before I ever wrote a line of code. Returning to graduate school was a deliberate choice to grow in a direction I had always been drawn to: understanding biology through computation. I am currently pursuing an M.S. in Statistics and Analytics (BioAnalytics) at the University of Arkansas, working in the Adams Analytics Group under Dr. Rich Adams. My thesis research involves assembling and analyzing chromosome-scale insect genomes and exploring how deep learning can help reconstruct evolutionary history from trait data. I am genuinely excited by the intersection of statistics, genomics, and machine learning, and I bring to that work a perspective grounded in real laboratory experience, careful scientific thinking, and a continuous drive to learn.
